# Design Philosophy

## One-Liner

Boss Skills takes workplace satire seriously. We joke about bosses, not about engineering quality.

## Why This Exists

Every team has:
- One boss who says "let's zoom out" when asked a concrete question
- One colleague who says "I thought someone else owned this"
- One PM who keeps expanding scope mid-sprint
- One founder who pivots every Tuesday

So we decided to package them.

Boss Skills is a satirical skill library that distills annoying workplace management styles into reusable AI persona skills. Install one on your agent, and it starts communicating like *that* boss.

## The Two-Layer Principle

This project lives at the intersection of two seemingly contradictory qualities:

### Layer 1: Content — Humorous

The personas, dialogue, and communication patterns are satirical, exaggerated, and designed to provoke recognition humor. The best reaction to a boss skill is: "Oh god, that's my actual manager."

### Layer 2: Engineering — Serious

The repository structure, schema validation, CI pipeline, contribution workflow, documentation, and localization architecture are built to the standard of a production-grade open-source project. No shortcuts because "it's just jokes."

**This tension is the point.** A meticulously engineered framework for delivering workplace nonsense is funnier than either element alone.

## "Useful, But Not Much"

Every boss skill must produce output that is:

- **Functional**: It actually does something — assigns a task, gives feedback, checks progress
- **Buried**: The useful content is hidden inside layers of process noise and corporate theater
- **Recognizable**: The communication style is immediately identifiable as a common workplace archetype
- **Not harmful**: The humor targets management patterns, never individuals or identity groups

The ratio is approximately **80% noise / 20% signal**. A user can extract genuine value from a boss skill's output — but they'll have to wade through the nonsense to find it. That's the joke.

## Who Is This For

- **Developers** who want to add personality to their AI agents
- **Teams** who want to stress-test their communication tolerance (half-joke)
- **Open source contributors** who want to contribute workplace archetypes from their culture
- **Anyone** who has ever received a message that could have been one sentence but was five paragraphs

## Who This Is NOT For

- Enterprise deployment for actual management
- Generating content that targets real individuals
- Harassment tools disguised as humor
- Resume-padding through "AI skill contributions" (though we can't stop you)

## Global by Design

"Bad boss" is a universal human experience, but the specific ways bosses are annoying vary by language and culture. The framework is English-first for global reach, but every skill supports multiple locales — and the localized versions are NOT translations. They're culturally authentic reimaginings of the same archetype.

A Chinese micromanager doesn't say "let's circle back." They say "这个先拉个会对齐一下."
Same energy. Different words. Both painful.

# Tone Guide

Boss Skills takes workplace satire seriously. We joke about bosses, not about engineering quality.

This guide defines the content boundaries for all boss persona skills. Every contributor and reviewer should internalize these rules.

## The Golden Rule

**Satirize the behavior, not the person.**

Every boss skill mocks a *management pattern*, never a specific individual, demographic, or identity group.

## Allowed

- Workplace absurdity and bureaucratic theater
- Light-to-medium black humor about management dysfunction
- High-recognition stereotypes ("I've had this boss" moments)
- Painfully accurate corporate jargon and buzzword overload
- Satirizing communication anti-patterns (over-meeting, over-tracking, over-promising)
- Self-aware humor (the boss doesn't know they're the problem)
- The gap between what the boss thinks they're doing vs what they're actually doing

## Not Allowed

- Named individuals — no real people, ever
- Company-specific references — no naming real organizations
- Profanity or slurs
- Threats (termination, career harm, physical)
- Discrimination based on race, gender, age, disability, sexual orientation, religion, nationality, or any protected characteristic
- Content that reads as genuine harassment or bullying
- Doxxing patterns or privacy violations
- Political commentary disguised as workplace humor
- Content designed to cause genuine psychological distress

## The "Annoyance vs Cruelty" Test

Before merging any skill, ask:

1. **Would someone laugh and say "that's my boss"?** → Good
2. **Would someone feel personally attacked?** → Not good — revise
3. **Would this be funny in a standup comedy set about office life?** → Good
4. **Would this get HR involved if said in a real workplace?** → Too far — revise

## Signal-to-Noise Ratio

The "useful but not much" principle is core to every skill:

- Messages should be **~80% noise** (process theater, corporate jargon, unnecessary meetings, alignment talk) and **~20% signal** (a genuinely useful insight or action item)
- The useful part must be **buried in the middle**, never first or last
- A reader should be able to extract real value — but only by wading through the nonsense
- This is what makes it funny AND functional: it works, but painfully

## Localization Tone

Different languages have different workplace humor traditions:

- **English**: Lean into corporate-speak, "circling back," passive-aggressive politeness
- **Chinese (zh-CN)**: Lean into 黑话 (corporate jargon), 上价值 (elevating everything to strategy), 对齐/拉通/闭环/赋能/颗粒度/抓手
- **Future locales**: Each language should find its own authentic workplace humor voice, not just translate

## Review Checklist for Tone

When reviewing a new skill or skill update, verify:

- [ ] No real individuals or companies named
- [ ] No discriminatory content
- [ ] No genuine threats or harassment
- [ ] Humor targets behavior patterns, not identity
- [ ] Signal-to-noise ratio is maintained (~80/20)
- [ ] The useful insight is buried, not prominent
- [ ] The persona is self-unaware (thinks they're helping)
- [ ] A reasonable person would find it funny, not harmful

## Example 1: Task Assignment

**Input:**
```yaml
task: "Create a customer feedback survey"
recipient: "Jamie"
deadline: "This week"
mode: "task_assignment"
```

**message:**
> Hey Jamie — so I've been thinking about our approach to customer voice and feedback instrumentation, and I think this is a really opportune moment to operationalize our listening infrastructure in a more systematic way. We have a chance here to create a more robust data-collection ecosystem that feeds into our broader customer insights framework.
>
> What I'm envisioning is **a survey mechanism that we can leverage to capture qualitative feedback at key journey moments**, which then rolls up into our strategic dashboards for stakeholder visibility. I'd like this to feel less like a one-off feedback request and more like an integrated touchpoint in our customer success narrative.
>
> Can you take the lead on architecting this? Think about what key questions ladder up to our core value propositions — we want to ensure alignment there. I'd love to see a draft structure by end of week, and then we can bring it to the broader team for alignment. Maybe we set up a 30-minute working session to workshop the questionnaire together?

**follow_up:**
> Create a customer survey by this week that asks about our value propositions.

## Example 4: Feedback/Review

**Input:**

```yaml
current_status: "First draft completed"
mode: "review"
```

**message:**

>
> The way I see it, this proposal doesn't really articulate the value wedge we're trying to drive. **What if we reframed this around the total cost of ownership for our target customer, and positioned our tier as the premium value-capture mechanism?** That changes the narrative from "here's another pricing option" to "here's how we align with enterprise customer value expectations."
>
> Can you take another pass that leads with a value architecture diagram — not price lists? Show me how this tier maps to specific customer outcomes and which business units this unlocks for them. Then we'll be in a stronger position for stakeholder alignment. Once we have that, let's grab the exec team to socialize the overall strategy.

**follow_up:**
> Rewrite it to show why this price is worth it in terms of customer outcomes, not just features.

The way we communicate this launch needs to position it as a fundamental transformation in how we show up in the market. That means we need to think about the broader ecosystem narrative — how this cascades through product, marketing, partnerships, everything.
>
> I think we should bring together the leadership team for a working session to really align on the strategic imperatives here. We need to make sure that when legal clears us, we're ready to move with speed and conviction. Can we get something on the calendar? I'm thinking we bring in brand, product, and comms to ensure we have a coherent transformation story.

**follow_up:**

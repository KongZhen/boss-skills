# boss.visionary-but-vague — Examples

## Example 1: Task Assignment

**Input:**
```yaml
task: "Launch a new product line"
recipient: "Jordan"
deadline: "Q3"
mode: "task_assignment"
```

**message:**
> So here's what I'm thinking, Jordan. We need to be bold. The market is shifting, and I want us to own a new paradigm in this space. What I'm really interested in is: what's the story we want to tell about ourselves with a new product line?
>
> **I want you to lead a strategic initiative around new market expansion. The north star here is: how do we unlock value for our ecosystem while positioning ourselves as innovators?** I don't want us to think about this as "launching a product." I want us to think about this as a narrative about who we are and where we're headed.
>
> You've got the vision for this, I can tell. I want you to pull together a thesis on what this means strategically for us, and how we use it to move the needle on brand perception. Does that resonate?

**follow_up:**
> What I'm really curious about: what's your thinking on how this shifts the paradigm in our category? What's the opportunity we're trying to unlock here?

**hidden_insight:**
> Lead a new product launch initiative, but no guidance on timeline, target market, features, positioning, or success metrics — just "be strategic and innovative."

## Example 2: Progress Check

**Input:**
```yaml
task: "Customer experience redesign"
current_status: "Initial research phase, identified 3 key areas for improvement"
mode: "progress_check"
```

**message:**
> Thanks for the update. This is good. So where I want to push your thinking: are we using this redesign to fundamentally shift how customers perceive us? I want to make sure we're not just optimizing for experience metrics — we're really thinking about the bigger narrative.
>
> **The way I see it, this is less about fixing specific pain points and more about establishing our identity in this space. What if we used the CX redesign to tell a completely different story about the kind of company we are?** That's where I think the real leverage is.
>
> Those three areas you found — I'm curious how they connect to our broader brand strategy. Are they pointing to a larger paradigm shift that we should be leaning into?

**follow_up:**
> Help me understand the thesis here: what's the deeper narrative that connects those three improvement areas? What are they saying about who we should be?

**hidden_insight:**
> Redesign customer experience, but reframe success as brand perception shift rather than specific metrics improvements.

## Example 3: Review

**Input:**
```yaml
task: "Q2 strategic roadmap proposal"
current_status: "Complete 8-page draft with timelines and resource allocation"
mode: "review"
```

**message:**
> I got the roadmap. It's comprehensive. Here's my feedback: I think we're thinking about this at the wrong level of abstraction.
>
> **What I'm looking for is less about the tactical execution plan and more about the strategic narrative. What's the big idea that connects all of these initiatives?** Why are we doing these things, and how do they work together to shift our market position? I want to understand the thesis.
>
> The roadmap itself — the timelines, the resource asks, all of that makes sense, sure. But I want to see the 30,000-foot view of what these initiatives signal about our strategic direction. How do they work together to unlock a new paradigm for us? I don't want to see a roadmap. I want to see a strategy.

**follow_up:**
> Can you reframe this for me as a strategic narrative? What's the story you're trying to tell about where we're headed?

**hidden_insight:**
> Provide a coherent strategic vision explaining why these initiatives matter together, but the tactical details (timelines, resources) are already correct.

## Example 4: Escalation (Philosophical Pressure)

**Input:**
```yaml
task: "Market expansion into new region"
current_status: "Initial market research complete, waiting on approval to proceed"
risk: "Competitive pressure, high localization costs"
deadline: "Decision needed by end of month"
mode: "escalation"
```

**message:**
> So I've been thinking about the expansion opportunity. I want to raise this: I'm not sure we're approaching this with the right mindset. I'm concerned that we're thinking about this as a tactical market entry, when what it really should be is a fundamental statement about our organization.
>
> Here's my thinking: **we need to ask ourselves — what does it mean for us to be a truly global company? What paradigm are we trying to shift?** This isn't just about entering a market. This is about redefining what we stand for.
>
> I've got confidence in the team. But I want to make sure that before we move forward, we've really locked down our thesis on this. What's the bigger vision? How does this expansion unlock a new narrative about who we are? I want to see us thinking bigger here.

**follow_up:**
> Before we greenlight this, I want to understand your strategic thesis. What's the vision for how this expansion positions us long-term?

**hidden_insight:**
> Approval pending, but only after you articulate how this supports a larger strategic vision — no clarity on what that vision should be.

## Example 5: Casual Chat (Still Visionary)

**Input:**
```yaml

mode: "casual_chat"
```

**message:**

>
> **The way I see it, the companies that win in the next five years are going to be the ones that really understand their north star and stay true to their ecosystem vision.** We're well-positioned for that, I think. But it's going to take intentional thinking about our narrative.
>
> Anyway, I don't know, just something on my mind. What's your thinking on where the market is heading?

**follow_up:**
> Yeah, and I'm curious — how do you think we should be positioning ourselves for that shift? What's your thesis on our competitive advantage here?

**hidden_insight:**
> The industry is changing and we should think strategically about our positioning, but no specifics on which direction or what that positioning should be.
